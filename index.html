From Medellín to the World

Born on July 13, 1991 — the symbolic day of the writing of Colombia's Constitution — in Medellín, Camila A. Chica Ortiz's life has been defined by an extraordinary breadth of purpose. A prodigious learner, she graduated high school at 16 and secured first place in admissions at the University of Antioquia, one of Colombia's most competitive public universities, where she began her journey in Political Science. She went on to earn her degree in Political Science with a GPA of 92/100 at Zion International University (ZIU-Online, Fort Lauderdale, FL), specializing in Public Administration with a focus on Freedom of Religion.

"She is building bridges between nations, generations, and disciplines — always with a heart set on truth, justice, innovation, and service."

Scholar, Writer & Political Analyst

Since moving to the United States in 2019, Camila has deepened her commitment to political science as both an academic and a practitioner. She continues her studies in Political Science at Zion International University (ZIU-Online, Fort Lauderdale) — specializing in Public Administration with a focus on Freedom of Religion — while actively publishing political analysis and commentary through her blog, Political Science's Studies.

Her written work analyzes political events, public policy, and civic governance through the lens of ethics, human rights, and religious freedom — values that define her political outlook and intellectual mission.

Global Advocate & Published Author

In 2023, Camila participated in the United Nations Congress on Freedom of Religion, Peace and Development in Geneva, Switzerland, joining prominent Colombian civic and spiritual leaders on a world stage. She co-authored Miraismo as part of the "Escuela de Pensamiento" at Zion International University — a work examining the ethical and civic foundations of political renewal. Separately, while leading research in Colombia, she authored a book on conflict resolution in Medellín (2016), grounded in her firsthand investigation of peace-building processes in the city. She also authored Procesos Artísticos y Culturales en la Comuna 1 de Medellín, published in partnership with the University of Antioquia.
